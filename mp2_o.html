Welcome to Elizabeth Rinkes's MP2: Digital Image

The following photographs are to demonstrate my knowledge gained through Adobe Photoshop to enhance digital images. In order to take both original photographs, I used my iPhone 6s.

Classroom Management
Before After

This photograph is of a bulletin board in my classroom that we use for "Classroom Jobs." I have removed the names of the students for privacy. For this photo, I began by resizing the photograph so that it is easier for viewers on the internet. I also cropped the photograph to get rid of unnecessary background noise. I wanted the colors of the water to pop, so I increased the saturation and contrast. This allowed the colors to be vibrant and really stand out in the image. I used the "Selection Tool" to select just the bulletin board, however, I wanted to isolate the concrete blocks around the bulletin board, so I used "Select Inverse." This allowed me to erase the concrete blocks using the "Eraser Tool" and put a blue background in place of them using the "Brush Tool." I wanted the colors to blend, so I also selected a blue color for the Brush Tool.

 

Everyday Life
Before After

This photograph is of my in-laws' cat. I loved their toy that they have for their cat and thought it would make for a fun, bright photograph. For this photo, I began by resizing the image to a more efficient size for the web as well as cropping the photo to allow the focus to remain on the object in the photo. Next, I used the "Quick Selection Tool" to help select the bright colors against the background. I used this tool to help distinguish between the two parts of the image. Keeping the image parts isolated, I used the "Dodge Tool" to enhance the colors of the cat and tunnels. Returning to the "Quick Selection Tool," I wanted to work on the background of the image so I used "Select Inverse." This allowed me to isolate the background only. I used the "Smudge" tool at a 78% and size 175. This allowed me to blur the background to allow the viewer to focus on the object in the photo. I then used "Auto Contrast" and "Auto Tone" to enhance the colors even further.
